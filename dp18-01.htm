Susan Kehaulani Halvorsen (R)

Hawaii State House - District 18

Non-incumbent candidate

Photo from Hawaii League of Women Voters

Born and raised in Honolulu, Susan graduated from Roosevelt High School and Brigham Young University-Hawaii.

She is a devoted wife and mother of 3 children and has served in many positions of responsibility for her community and church.

Susan has served the people of Hawaii for over 12 years as a Social Worker with the Department of Health.

She works with and assists Kūpuna and the disabled to receive necessary health care. She also helps to obtain permanent homes for many of Hawaii’s homeless and resolves escalating situations in the community to protect the public.

Susan is experienced in collaborating with federal, state, and private organizations to benefit individuals and families.

She serves as Union Steward for the Hawaii Employees Government Association (HGEA) Unit 13 in the Department of Health.

Natalie J. Iwasa

Honolulu City Council - District 4

Non-incumbent candidate

Photo from Hawaii League of Women Voters

For over four years I have been attending City Council meetings and testifying on a variety of issues. I have seen politically-motivated decisions and legislation being passed that was self-serving, at the expense of the taxpayer.

About 11 years ago, I started my own CPA practice, and that gave me the flexibility to become active in the community. The first time I testified was in 2004 in support of keeping agriculture in Kamilonui Valley. I joined Livable Hawaii Kai Hui and regularly attended meetings of the Hawaii Kai Neighborhood Board. I found out that my input made a difference. I am currently a member of the board and chair of the Planning and Zoning Committee.

For the last few years, I have attended nearly all the full City Council meetings and have advocated for more sensible spending of our money. It is my participation in these meetings that has raised my interest in taking on yet a bigger role – that of councilmember.

Trevor Ozawa

Honolulu City Council - District 4

Non-incumbent candidate

Photo from Hawaii League of Women Voters

Trevor grew up in Hawaii Kai and currently resides there with his wife, Nietzsche. Trevor is an attorney, specializing in civil litigation while Nietzsche serves as a Deputy Public Defender for the State of Hawaii.

Trevor is one of four kids from a working class family. Trevor’s late father was a chef and his mother worked at Aloha Airlines for 34 years before becoming a local small business owner. From an early age, Trevor's parents instilled the values of hard work, commitment, and responsibility. At the age of 15, Trevor got his first job at Waialae Country Club. Trevor practiced golf on days that he was not working and soon became an avid lover of the game.

Trevor attended Kamiloiki Elementary before attending Kamehameha Schools. He received his Bachelor’s Degree in Economics from the University of Southern California and thereafter received his law degree from Suffolk University Law School.

Carl Strouble

Honolulu City Council - District 4

Non-incumbent candidate

Photo from Hawaii League of Women Voters

I have been in Hawaii for 30 years, shortly after my enlistment in the U.S. Navy. I've seen friends struggle to make a living here, only to leave for it has become increasingly hard to do. Those friends were born and raised here, they now only have hopes to return. I've chosen to run for city council to make a difference, by working on solutions immediately. This City Council needs to do more for it's people in fulfilling its role as public servant. To keep the focus and thoughts about the people, before themselves.

Tommy Waters

Honolulu City Council - District 4

Non-incumbent candidate

Cropped from Civil Beat photo

I served three terms in the Hawaii State House of Representatives. I served as the Chairman of the Judiciary Committee, Chairman of the Committee on Higher Education as well as the Vice Chair of the Environmental Protection Committee. It was my duty to oversee and approve the entire budget of the University of Hawaii System statewide as well as the Budget for the Judiciary statewide. I took my job seriously, and worked hard to improve the services these important institutions represent as well as to ensure that every dollar was spent wisely. I promise to do the same at Honolulu City Hall.
